Integrity

As a missionary in Torre Vieja, Spain, a friend of mine looked me in the eyes and said with deep conviction, "Quiero ser un hombre integro como usted", that is to say, "I want to be a man of integrity like you" .  Though I was deeply flattered by his compliment as a man of integrity, I still felt I didn't know what it meant.  As I look at it today, integrity is ambitiously attaining your good desires by honest and moral means.  In order to achieve what you want, you must deserve it and rightfully so.  

FOCUS

I remember a season before finals in my studies at BYU when I had studied every night untill 2 am and went to bed only to wake up in the morning to start the process all over.  Late one night I just had it.  Deeply discouraged, I reconsidered my life choices and sought other more livable options.  In my frustration and tears I passed a big dumpster on my way home from campus.  As I stopped to take a few minutes to look at the dumpster I started to imagine all the things I could throw away: books, backpack, laptop, myself.  In my thoughts, I realized what I really wanted to throw away.  I wanted to throw away the "fluff" of life.  I realized that if we firmly focus on our end goal our actions become more focused, the "fluff" is swept asside.  As we do this, our achievements become more concentrated, meaningful, and rewarding.  I realized that success is not founded in meaningless tasks and checking boxes; its about becoming one capable of solving lifes hardest challenges.
